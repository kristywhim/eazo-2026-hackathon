📋 Scoring Criteria

**Five dimensions. 10 points each. 50 points total.**
Score each dimension independently through the judging system — totals are calculated automatically.

Each judge scores independently. The regional score is the average across all judges. Individual scores are not disclosed publicly.

---

### 01 · Product Completeness
Real and usable — not just a demo.

> Is this a working product a real user could pick up and use on their own?

| Score | Standard |
|-------|----------|
| 9–10 | Fully live and functional. Core user flow works end-to-end with no critical gaps. A real user could use it independently right now. |
| 7–8 | Main features are complete. Minor rough edges or edge cases, but the product is genuinely usable. |
| 5–6 | Core flow mostly works but has noticeable incomplete sections. Requires guidance to navigate. |
| 3–4 | Prototype stage. Most features don't work or haven't been built yet. |
| 1–2 | Concept or mockup only. No interactive, live product exists. |

---

### 02 · Innovation
Is the approach or solution genuinely new.

> Does this bring a fresh perspective or novel solution to the problem?

| Score | Standard |
|-------|----------|
| 9–10 | Approach is clearly original. Nothing quite like it exists in the market. Genuinely surprising. |
| 7–8 | Clear differentiation from existing solutions — not a straight copy with minor tweaks. |
| 5–6 | Some original thinking, but closely resembles existing products with limited added value. |
| 3–4 | Reimplementation of a common solution. No meaningful new angle. |
| 1–2 | Direct clone of an existing product. No original contribution. |

---

### 03 · Technical Execution
Depth and quality of Eazo Creator usage.

> Has the team used Eazo Creator skillfully? How deep and stable is the implementation?

| Score | Standard |
|-------|----------|
| 9–10 | Creative and advanced use of Eazo Creator. Complex data logic or interactions implemented. Pushes beyond basic platform capabilities. |
| 7–8 | Platform features used thoughtfully. Clear product architecture. Functions reliably. |
| 5–6 | Basic implementation. Platform capabilities underutilized. Execution is surface-level. |
| 3–4 | Only the most basic platform features used. Thin product structure. |
| 1–2 | Minimal platform usage. Product has almost no real logic or function. |

---

### 04 · Design & Experience
Clear interface, intuitive product.

> Is it comfortable to use? Is the interface clear and the experience smooth?

| Score | Standard |
|-------|----------|
| 9–10 | Polished UI, smooth interactions, clear information hierarchy. Users can understand the product without guidance. |
| 7–8 | Good overall experience. Visual consistency throughout. Minor details could be improved but don't impede use. |
| 5–6 | Functional but average. Noticeable information confusion or interaction gaps present. |
| 3–4 | Rough interface. Interaction logic is unclear. Requires significant guidance to operate. |
| 1–2 | Poor experience. Confusing or undesigned interface that undermines the product's perceived value. |

---

### 05 · Commercial Potential
Real market and real user need.

> Is there a genuine user need here? Could this become a real business?

| Score | Standard |
|-------|----------|
| 9–10 | Clear target user, real pain point, and viable commercial path. Supported by data or early user feedback. |
| 7–8 | User need is well-defined. Market direction is reasonable. Some early thinking on monetization. |
| 5–6 | Problem is visible but target user is vague. Commercial path is unclear. |
| 3–4 | Niche or unclear demand. Limited commercial value. |
| 1–2 | No clear application or market. Hard to see a real use case. |

---

💡 Scoring Tips

**Anchor at 6**: Treat 6 as "meets the basic standard for this competition." Scores of 7+ mean the team exceeded expectations. 9–10 is reserved for genuinely standout work in that dimension. Avoid clustering scores at 8–10 or 5–6 — differentiation is what makes scoring meaningful.

**Score independently**: Please do not discuss specific scores with other judges before the session ends. Independent scoring is what gives the results validity.

**Impression vs. dimension**: If your overall impression is positive but one dimension is weak, score that dimension honestly. Don't use high scores to mask weak areas — the weighted composite will reflect overall quality accurately.

**Time is limited**: If you have questions about a project, jot down a keyword or two and return to it after all Demos. Don't let it disrupt the current rhythm.

---

📊 Score Weight (for context)

Judge scores (J) account for **40%** of the final composite score, alongside public votes (50%) and peer review (10%). The quality and spread of judge scores have a significant impact on final rankings.
